2.4 Pathology of Intracranial Haemorrhage 2/4

Help References

Both extradural haematomas and subdural haematomas are associated with head injuries.

Question

The image above is of the upper surface of the right cerebral hemisphere post-mortem with the dura mater and haemorrhage reflected towards the left. What does this picture show?

Select the answer you think is correct.

a. a subarachnoid haemorrhage

b. a subdural haemorrhage

c. an extradural haemorrhage
Sorry, that’s not right. The picture shows an acute subdural haematoma sustained by a woman who fell from her horse. Blood clot is seen on the inferior surface of the dura (the dura has been reflected) and so the haematoma lies between the dura and the brain. A subarachnoid haemorrhage would lie beneath the arachnoid that closely invests the surface of the brain and would be seen on the surface of the brain in this illustration. An extradural haematoma would be outside the dura and would not be seen in this view.
