The optic nerve and ophthalmic artery both leave the skull through the optic canal. All the other answers are correct.
  • The arachnoid bridges over the sulci; cisternae are formed where the arachnoid and pia are widely separated.
  • All structures passing to and from the brain must cross the subarachnoid space.
  • Arachnoid projects into venous sinuses to form arachnoid granulations or villi. CSF is absorbed into the blood through arachnoid granulations.

Arachnoid granulations and arachnoid villi or Pacchionian bodies were described by Pacchioni in the 18th century. They are specialised structures consisting of leptomeningeal cells, coated by venous endothelium. They project into the venous sinuses in the skull.

Arachnoid granulations and arachnoid villi are not present before birth; they develop and gradually increase in size to become lobulated and complex in adults. Arachnoid granulations are 1-2mm, villi are smaller.

The diagram on the next page shows a longitudinal section through a granulation. Arachnoid protrudes through the dura mater to form the neck of the granulation. A core of subarachnoid channels is central and separated by collagen trabeculae. A cap of leptomeningeal cells is coated by endothelium of the dural venous sinus.

Cerebrospinal fluid is transported from the subarachnoid space into the venous blood by a bulk flow across the venous endothelial cells.
