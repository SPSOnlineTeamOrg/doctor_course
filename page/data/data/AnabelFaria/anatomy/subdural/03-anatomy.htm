2.3 Anatomy relevant to subdural haematoma 1/12
Help References

A subdural haematoma results from the tearing of a vein that drains into a dural venous sinus. The brain and spinal cord are surrounded by 3 meninges: dura mater, arachnoid, pia mater. The arachnoid and pia mater are known as leptomeninges. The arachnoid is separated from the dura by a potential space (subdural space) and from the pia mater by the subarachnoid space.
