2.5 Complications 1/7

Help References

The major complications of subdural haematoma are raised intracranial pressure and displacement of the brain. The intracranial cavity is divided into 3 major compartments by dural sheets.


Question

The image above is a view of the tentorium cerebelli from below; part of the brain below the tentorium has been removed. Within the skull, which of the following does the tentorium cerebelli separate?

Select the answer you think is correct.

a. the two cerebral hemispheres
b. the brain from the dura
c. the cerebral hemispheres from the cerebellum
Sorry, that’s not right. Within the skull, the tentorium cerebelli separates the cerebral hemispheres from the cerebellum. In the image, the tentorium cerebelli, seen from below, is on the under surface of the cerebral hemispheres. The lower brain stem and cerebellum have been removed to show how the mid-brain occupies the opening in the tentorium cerebelli. In this specimen the lateral aspects of the tentorium cerebelli have been cut free from the petrous temporal bones in the skull. On the left-hand side of the picture, the medial aspect of the temporal lobe is just visible through the tentorial opening between the free edge of the tentorium and the mid-brain.
