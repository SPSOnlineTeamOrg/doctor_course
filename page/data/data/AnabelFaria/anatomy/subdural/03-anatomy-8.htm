2.3 Anatomy relevant to subdural haematoma 8/12

Help References

The cavernous sinuses lie on either side of the sella turcica (pituitary fossa) that houses the pituitary gland. Both cavernous sinuses extend from the Superior Orbital fissures (Anteriorly) to the petorus part of the temporal bone (posteriorly).

This is very important clinically, as infections from the face can spread into the skull, resulting in cavernous sinus thrombosis.

Question

Which of the following structures do you think are related to the cavernous sinus? Click on each one in turn to see if you are right.

a. Carotid artery

Correct, the internal carotid artery with sympathetics wrapped around it pass through the sinus

b. Abducent nerve

Correct, it passes through the sinus together with the internal carotid artery

c. Facial nerve

Incorrect, the facial nerve emerges from the pons and enters the internal auditory meatus

d. Oculomotor nerve

Correct, it is found in the lateral wall of the sinus

e. Trochlear nerve

Correct, it is found in the lateral wall of the sinus

f. Ophthalmic nerve

Correct, it lies on the lateral wall of the sinus

g. Maxillary nerve

Correct, it is found in the lateral wall of the sinus

h. Mandibular nerve

Incorrect, the mandibular nerve enters foramen ovale, further away from the cavernous sinus
