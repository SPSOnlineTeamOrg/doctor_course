The venous dural sinuses are situated between the two layers of dura mater. They are lined by endothelium and contain no valves.


Question

Think for a moment and consider why there are no valves in the dural sinuses.

The blood pressure in venules and veins of medium size is low – it cannot oppose the force of gravity. Valves are therefore only required in the veins of the limbs, to prevent the backflow of blood. Veins in the head and neck, veins from the viscera or great veins do not contain valves.
