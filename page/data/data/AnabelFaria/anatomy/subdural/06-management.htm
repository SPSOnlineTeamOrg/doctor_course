2.6 Management and Treatment

Help References

Treatment of the subdural haematoma will depend upon its size and chronicity.

  • If small and causing no clinical symptoms, the subdural haematoma may be treated conservatively without surgery and will eventually resolve and disappear.
  • If the volume is large enough to cause clinical symptoms, then surgical evacuation is more likely to be required.
  • In cases of acute subdural haematoma, the clot cannot be evacuated through burr holes and a craniotomy is usually required.
  • When the haematoma is chronic, it will have become partly liquefied and can be evacuated through burr holes. The formation of granulation tissue on the surfaces of the haematoma may make it adherent to the surface of the dura mater rendering the evacuation more difficult and often requiring multiple burr holes.
