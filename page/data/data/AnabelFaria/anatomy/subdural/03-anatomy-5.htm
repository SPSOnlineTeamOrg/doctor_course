The superior sagital sinus grooves the vault of the skull and flows into the confluence of sinuses, situated at the internal occipital protuberance. The superior sagital sinus is usually continuous with the right transverse sinus.

The superior cerebral veins drain into the superior sagital sinus. It is the rupture of these veins that cause a subdural haemorrhage (haematoma).
