Question

The leptomeningeal blood vessels in the image above are stained by thioflavin and viewed in UV light. Which of the following do you think this image shows?

Select the answer you think is correct.

a. atherosclerosis
b. haemorrhage
c. cerebral amyloid angiopathy with amyloid in the vessel walls
Sorry, that's not right. The fluorescent deposits of amyloid are between the smooth muscle cells in the basement membranes of the vessels. It is the basement membranes that are the channels along which interstitial fluid and soluble Aβ travel as they drain along artery walls – effectively the “lymphatic drainage” of the brain. As the amount of insoluble Aβ in the vessel wall increases, it may block further drainage of soluble Aβ and interstitial fluid from the brain and also weakens the vessel wall that may rupture and result in intracerebral haemorrhage.
