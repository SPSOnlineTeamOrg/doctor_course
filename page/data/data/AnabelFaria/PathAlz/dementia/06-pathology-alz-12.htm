4.6c Pathology: Alzheimer's Disease 12/12

The brain has no conventional lymphatics so interstitial fluid and solutes drain from the brain along the walls of capillaries and arteries using basement membranes as conduits. With increasing age, elimination of Aβ along perivascular pathways fails, probably due to age changes in the blood vessel walls. Aβ accumulates as insoluble amyloid fibrils in the blood vessel walls as cerebral amyloid angiopathy and this probably further impedes the elimination of soluble Aβ from the brain. Aβ accumulates as insoluble plaques in the brain inducing a certain amount of damage and microglial and astrocyte proliferation. As dementia ensues there is a build up of soluble Aβ; this may itself be toxic to neurons or the accumulation of other soluble metabolites in the brain parenchyma may interfere with neuronal function and cognitive decline.
