Aβ also accumulates in the walls of blood vessels (Cerebral Amyloid Angiopathy). The temporal lobes and the hippocampus are the earliest and most severely involved areas of the brain in Alzheimer’s Disease, but other areas such as the frontal and occipital lobes also accumulate amyloid plaques and neurofibrillary tangles.

The section of brain here is stained by haematoxylin and eosin. The two pink circular structures in the picture are blood vessels with amyloid in their walls. This is the appearance of cerebral amyloid angiopathy. The next section of the course deals with the Pathogenesis of Alzheimer's Disease and explains how Aβ is produced by neurons in the brain and drains with interstitial fluid along blood vessels walls. In Alzheimer's Disease, the Aβ becomes entrapped in the vessel wall.
