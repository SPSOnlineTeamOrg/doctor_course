This section from the brain of a patient with Alzheimer's Disease shows fluorescent plaque of amyloid. The section is stained by the dye, thioflavin, which attaches to the amyloid fibrils (11nm diameter) in such a way that when it is viewed in ultraviolet light (UV) under the microscope, amyloid is fluorescent.
