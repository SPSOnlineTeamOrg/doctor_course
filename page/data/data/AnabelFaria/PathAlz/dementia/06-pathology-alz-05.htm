The section of brain from a patient with Alzheimer's Disease shown here is stained for amyloid β (Aβ) (brown). Staining shows a dense core of amyloid surrounded by a diffuse halo representing a senile plaque.

At the top and bottom of the picture are two of the classical senile plaques found in the cerebral cortex of patients with Alzheimer's Disease. The dense cores of amyloid are surrounded by diffuse regions of insoluble Aβ. At the bottom right of the picture is more diffuse plaque with no central dense core of amyloid.
