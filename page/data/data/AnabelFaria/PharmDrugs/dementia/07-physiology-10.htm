Question

Cholinesterase inhibitors are currently used for the treatment of Alzheimer's disease.

Read the following statements and decide if they are true or false.

1. The use of cholinesterase inhibitors will prevent cholinergic neurones from degenerating.      True False

Incorrect!
Correct!

2. Cholinesterase inhibitors will result in increased activation of ACh receptors     True False

Correct!
Incorrect!

3. The use of cholinesterase inhibitors will increase the synthesis of ACh by cholinergic neurons.     True False

Incorrect!
Correct!
Cholinesterase inhibitors will result in increased activation of ACh receptors.

Cholinesterase inhibitors slow the hydrolysis of ACh by acetylcholinesterase thereby prolonging the time that ACh is available to activate receptors. There is no evidence that the use of cholinesterase inhibitors prevents the ongoing degeneration of cholinergic neurons in Alzheimer’s disease, or that the synthesis of ACh is increased by these drugs.
