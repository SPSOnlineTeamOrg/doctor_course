4.0 Dementia: Introduction

Help References

At the end of this section you will be able to:

  1. Understand the clinical definition and clinical features of dementia
  2. Appreciate that the major pathological features in dementias are :
    (a) Intracellular accumulation of the proteins tau or synuclein
    (b) Extracellular accumulation of amyloid proteins in brain tissue and in the walls of cerebral blood vessels
  3. Understand the clinical features of dementias and the principles by which different types of dementia are distinguished both clinically and pathologically
  4. Appreciate the social and economic costs of dementia
  5. Recognise the role of genetic factors as causes and risk factors for Alzheimer’s disease.
  6. Understand the anatomy of the limbic system and its relevance to dementias
  7. Appreciate the normal structure of neurons
  8. Understand how failure of disposal of tau and synuclein leads to their accumulation within neurons in dementias
  9. Recognise that Amyloid-β (Aβ) accumulates in Interstitial Fluid drainage pathways in the walls of blood vessel (effectively the lymphatics of the brain) and that this probably impairs the elimination of Amyloid-β from the elderly and Alzheimer brain
  10. Understand the synthesis and release of acetyl choline by neurons and how cholinergic transmission functions
  11. Appreciate the principles involved in treating Alzheimer’s disease with anticholinesterases
  12. Understand the management of Alzheimer’s disease and how knowledge of the pathology Alzheimer’s disease leads to the development of new treatments.
