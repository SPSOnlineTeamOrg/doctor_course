ACh synthesis and packaging

Acetylcholine is a small molecule is the only small molecule neurotransmitter that is not an amino acid metabolite. As with all small molecule transmitters, it is synthesised in the axon terminals after which it is packaged into vesicles in preparation for release. Click here to see an animation of this. Take note of A and B below as they relate to the animation.

(A) ACh is synthesised from choline and acetyl coenzyme A (acetyl CoA) by the enzyme choline acetyltransferase (ChAT). Choline, which cannot be synthesised in the nervous system, is obtained from dietary sources and taken up into cholinergic neurons through a high-affinity choline uptake transporter. Acetyl CoA is produced as a metabolite of several metabolic pathways. ChAT transfers the acetyl group from acetyl CoA to the choline producing ACh and CoA.

(B) ACh is packaged into vesicles for eventual release by the vesicular ACh transporter, which exchanges ACh for a proton. The concentration of ACh within the vesicles can be as high as 500-1000mM.
