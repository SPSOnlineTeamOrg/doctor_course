Five subtypes of muscarinic receptors (termed m1-m5) have been identified. All muscarinic receptors are metabotropic, utilising G-proteins for signal transduction. The m1 subtype, is the predominant muscarinic receptor in the CNS and is located on the postsynaptic terminal. M1 utilises the Gq/11 G-protein which, following ACh binding, activates the enzyme phospholipase C, which in turn stimulates the production of two intracellular second messengers – inositol 1,4,5, trisphosphate (IP3) and diacylglycerol (DAG). IP3 stimulates calcium release from intracellular stores whilst DAG stimulates protein phosphorylation through activation of protein kinase enzymes. Activation of m1 receptors therefore results in a complex response in the post-synaptic neurone.
