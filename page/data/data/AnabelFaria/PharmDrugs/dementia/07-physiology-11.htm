4.7c Pharmacology of neural connections & dementia 11/13

Help References

Alzheimer’s Disease primarily affects the cholinergic pathways within the cortex and limbic system resulting in a progressive, irreversible loss of cholinergic neurons. In addition, there is a significant decrease in the function of surviving cholinergic neurons through decreased ACh synthesis, release and post-synaptic signalling. Taken together, these two factors result in a significant reduction in cholinergic transmission in the affected areas. Because of this, the major strategy for pharmacological therapy in AD has been the development of compounds to enhance cholinergic transmission using inhibitors of acetylcholinesterase.
