4.7a Physiology & Pharmacology: Cholinergic Pathways
Help References

Cholinergic neurons located in the forebrain and brain stem have diffuse projections to the cortex and hippocampus. As a result, cholinergic terminals are widely distributed throughout the brain. The main cholinergic input to the cortex comes from the nucleus basalis of Meynert which is located in the basal forebrain. The septo-hippocampal tract is formed from cholinergic cells arising in the septal nuclei projecting to the hippocampus. Degeneration of these pathways has been shown to be important in the pathogenesis of Alzheimer’s Disease. In addition to these pathways, there are large numbers of cholinergic interneurons, particularly in the striatum.
