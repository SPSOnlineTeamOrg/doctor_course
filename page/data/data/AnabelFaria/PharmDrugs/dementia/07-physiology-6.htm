Question

If the extracellular concentration of potassium ions ([K+]e) is increased , what effect will this have on cholinergic neurons?

Select the answer you think is correct.

a. the membrane potential will become hyperpolarised
b. less acetylcholine will be released with each action potential
c. the neuron will become depolarised
Sorry, that's not right. The concentration of potassium ions is much higher in the cytoplasm than in the extracellular fluid. Increasing the [K+]e will therefore reduce the concentration gradient for potassium across the cell membrane and result in a depolarisation of the neuron (movement of the membrane potential towards 0mV). This will result in neurons becoming more excitable, more likely to generate an action potential and therefore more likely to release neurotransmitter.

Question

Which of these will NOT increase the amount of neurotransmitter that is released in response to an action potential?

Select the answer you think is correct.

a. activation of more presynaptic ACh receptors
b. opening more presynaptic voltage-dependent calcium channels
c. activation of more postsynaptic ACh receptors
Sorry, that's not right. Neurotransmitter release is totally dependent on the entry of calcium into the presynaptic terminal, and the amount of transmitter released is related to the amount of calcium present. Increasing the number of presynaptic ACh receptors (α7 nicotinic type) and voltage-dependent calcium channels that are activated will in turn increase the concentration of calcium in the presynaptic terminal leading to increased transmitter release. In contrast, ACh acting on postsynaptic receptors will not influence transmitter release from the presynaptic terminal.
